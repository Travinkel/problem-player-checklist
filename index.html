Problem Player Checklist

Item Consistently (2 points) Some of the time (1 point) Does not apply (0 points)
Rule Disregarding Consistently ignores game rules or refuses to learn them, affecting gameplay and progression.
Spotlight Hogging Frequently interrupts others and monopolizes game time, preventing fellow players from participating or developing their characters.
Aggressive Behavior Displays hostility towards other players or the DM, whether in character or out, including verbal attacks or threats.
Lack of Teamwork Acts in self-interest or makes decisions that harm the group's cohesion, goals, or survival without reasonable in-game justification.
Metagaming Regularly uses out-of-character knowledge to influence in-character decisions, undermining game integrity.
Unreliable Participation Frequently misses sessions without notice or reasonable explanation, disrupting campaign continuity and planning.
Cheating Attempts to manipulate dice rolls, character stats, or other game elements dishonestly.
Disrespect for the Game Shows contempt for the game setting, story, or the efforts of the DM and other players, diminishing the group's enjoyment.
Boundary Violation Disregards other players' comfort zones with inappropriate content or actions, despite previous feedback or established session zero agreements.
Conflict Instigation Deliberately provokes in-game or out-of-game conflicts among players or between players and the DM, creating a hostile environment.
Refusal to Engage Shows apathy or unwillingness to participate in roleplaying, combat, or decision-making, dragging down the group's energy.
Over-Negotiation Constantly argues with the DM's decisions or game outcomes, slowing down the game and challenging the DM's authority excessively.
Lack of Preparation Comes to sessions without necessary materials, preparation, or recollection of previous events, requiring constant catch-up.
Disruptive Behavior Engages in behaviors that break game immersion or distract from the session, such as unrelated conversation, phone use, or inappropriate humor.
Resistance to Feedback Responds negatively to constructive criticism or suggestions for improvement from the DM or fellow players.
Inflexible Roleplaying Refuses to adapt their character's behavior even when it's clearly causing issues within the party or disrupting the narrative flow, insisting on "it's what my character would do" to the detriment of group harmony.
Loot Dominance Always insists on taking the best loot or negotiating to get more than their fair share, without regard for the group's needs or fairness.
Gameplay Sabotage Makes in-game decisions that knowingly sabotage missions, plans, or strategies the group has agreed upon, for personal amusement or gain.
Lack of Respect for Game Time Consistently shows up late to sessions, takes extended breaks, or otherwise disrespects the collective time commitment of the group.
Failure to Engage with the Story Shows little interest in the campaign's story, lore, or NPCs, and does not contribute to creating a shared narrative experience, often derailing plot points for personal entertainment.
